Title: Solid-State Electrolytes for Lithium Batteries
Author: Alice Zhang
Affiliation: Materials Science Seminar
Date: 2026-05-15

1. Research Background
- Lithium metal batteries require safer electrolytes.
- Liquid electrolytes raise leakage and flammability concerns.
- Solid-state electrolytes can improve safety and energy density.

2. Methodology
- Compare sulfide, oxide, and polymer electrolyte families.
- Evaluate ionic conductivity, interfacial resistance, and processability.
- Summarize characterization methods and common failure modes.

3. Key Results
- Sulfide electrolytes show high conductivity but need moisture control.
- Oxide electrolytes offer stability but can be hard to densify.
- Polymer electrolytes are processable but usually need thermal assistance.

4. Conclusion
- Interface engineering is the central bottleneck.
- Hybrid electrolytes may balance conductivity, stability, and manufacturability.
